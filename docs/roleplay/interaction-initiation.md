# Interaction/Initiation Guidelines

All hostile roleplay must have proper interaction and valid initiation before any violence or escalation takes place. Players are expected to create realistic and meaningful scenes instead of immediately resorting to shootings or robberies.

Hostile interactions must contain at least two minutes of roleplay before escalating into violence unless the situation reasonably calls for immediate action.

Initiation must be clear and give the opposing party enough time to understand the situation and respond accordingly. Immediate violence without proper roleplay may be considered RDM.

Hostile roleplay should always have realistic reasoning behind it. Randomly targeting players, creating low-effort scenes, or looking for excuses to start conflict is not allowed.

Civilians are expected to have proper build-up before escalating situations into robberies or violent interactions. Meaningful roleplay and repeated negative interactions should exist before major escalation occurs.

Third-partying active scenes without valid involvement is prohibited. Players should not interfere with ongoing situations unless they are directly connected to the roleplay or personally know the individuals involved within character.

Players may not join scenes simply because they hear gunshots, see police activity, or are informed through out-of-character communication.

Once a scene begins, all players are expected to prioritize quality roleplay over winning the interaction. Unrealistic escalation, baiting reactions, or intentionally disrupting scenes may result in punishment.
