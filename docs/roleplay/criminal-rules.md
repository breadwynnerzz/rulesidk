# Criminal Rules

## Kill or Rob

Players may either kill or rob during a hostile interaction, but not both within the same scene.

If a player is fully downed, they may no longer be robbed. Robbery scenarios must remain active and roleplay-focused while the victim is conscious.

Killing a player after robbing them, or robbing a player after intentionally downing them, is not allowed.

## Targeting

Repeatedly targeting the same player, group, or organization without valid roleplay reasoning is not allowed.

All hostile actions must be backed by meaningful roleplay and believable motives. Personal dislike, profit alone, or looking for constant conflict is not considered valid reasoning.

## Hostages

Hostages must be taken through proper force and roleplay interaction. Asking someone to "volunteer" as a hostage is not considered valid roleplay.

Hostages are expected to value their life and cooperate realistically during situations. Players may not abuse hostage situations to create unrealistic demands or intentionally stall scenes.

## Robberies

All robberies must contain proper interaction and realistic escalation before any items are taken. Players are expected to create meaningful roleplay instead of rushing scenes for profit.

Only items physically carried by the player may be taken during robberies. Forcing players to transfer money, access private storage, or hand over Tebex-related items is prohibited.

## Scamming

Scamming is allowed within reasonable limits as long as it remains realistic and roleplay-driven.

Scams involving Tebex items, real-money purchases, vehicles, houses, or anything considered excessively valuable are not allowed. Players may not use scams as an excuse to avoid proper interaction or create low-quality roleplay.

## Vehicle Use During Crime

Vehicles should be used realistically during criminal activity. Using vehicles that create unfair advantages or unrealistic escape scenarios is not allowed.

Motorcycles, dirt bikes, mini imports, or similar vehicles may not be used for major crimes, organized shootouts, or large-scale robberies unless specifically approved by server rules.

## Appearance Changes

Players may not completely alter their appearance during or immediately after criminal activity to avoid identification or consequences.

Minor changes such as clothing adjustments are allowed, but drastically changing your character's appearance during active situations is considered unrealistic.

## Heist Participation Limits

Large-scale robberies and heists are limited to approved participant counts. Any player directly assisting, scouting, transporting, or participating in the scene counts toward the total limit.

Joining active heists late or remaining nearby waiting to become involved is not allowed.

## Hitman Roleplay

Hitman or contract killing roleplay is not permitted within Brick City RP.

This type of roleplay often creates unrealistic interactions and low-quality scenes that negatively impact the server environment.

## Shooting From Vehicles

Shooting from vehicles is heavily restricted and may only be used in limited situations for disabling purposes.

Players are expected to properly exit vehicles before engaging in extended gunfights or lethal combat. Vehicle combat should not replace meaningful roleplay interaction.

## Vehicle Interaction Rules

Players may defend their vehicle if someone is actively attempting to steal or tamper with it through roleplay.

Using vehicles to intentionally ram players, force interactions, or initiate hostile roleplay is not allowed and may be considered VDM.

## Drug Activity

Drug-related roleplay must follow intended server mechanics and realistic interaction standards.

Abusing NPC mechanics, exploiting locations, or using unintended methods to gain advantages during drug activity is strictly prohibited and may result in punishment.
