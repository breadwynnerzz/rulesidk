# PD / EMS Conduct

Law enforcement and EMS are core parts of the server and are expected to maintain high-quality, realistic, and professional roleplay at all times. Players within whitelisted departments are held to a higher standard due to the impact they have on the overall server environment and player experience.

Abuse of authority, favoritism, bias, corruption outside approved roleplay, toxic behavior, or intentionally creating low-quality scenes is strictly prohibited. Department members are expected to remain fair, mature, and professional during all interactions both in-character and out-of-character.

Police officers are expected to follow proper escalation, realistic procedures, and reasonable force during situations. Officers may not rush scenes, force outcomes, or escalate situations unnecessarily simply to "win" interactions. Unrealistic policing, excessive force without roleplay justification, baiting players into conflict, or intentionally seeking shootouts is not allowed.

EMS personnel are expected to prioritize medical roleplay and patient care during scenes. Injuries should be treated seriously and realistically whenever possible. EMS may not rush medical scenes, ignore injuries without reason, revive players carelessly, or avoid roleplay for convenience.

Both departments are expected to prioritize server health and roleplay quality over personal gain or ego. Members should focus on creating enjoyable and believable interactions for everyone involved instead of treating scenes like competitive gameplay.

Whitelisted departments are privileges, not guaranteed positions. Failure to meet expectations, repeated low-quality roleplay, inactivity, abuse of permissions, unrealistic conduct, poor professionalism, or violations of server rules may result in strikes, suspension, demotion, blacklist, or permanent termination from the department.
